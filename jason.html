Jason Dooley

Student Of Code

Recently retired from the U.S. Army after almost 15 years of service, I have set my sights on a career in Software Engineering after realizing that a job doesn't come easy just because you have the title "Veteran" next to your name. I have come to realize that "skills pay the bills".

After being honorably discharged and given medical retirement from the Army, I spent the last 14 months looking for viable employment. I tried to sell solar panels for two weeks. I worked as a mail carrier for a month. Finally, I found myself working as a hardware technician at Google. It was here that I realized my calling is on the software side. I want to build resources for military veterans that suffer from PTSD. My longterm goal is to build a company that specializes in finding resources for those with PTSD and providing a community that enables meetings, camaraderie, and shows the Veterans that somebody cares about them.

In my free time (which is slim to none with a 10 month old baby at home), I enjoy flying my DJI Phantom 3 drone, watching sports on television, and spending time with my family. When not doing any of those things, I am coding, honing my newfound skills and craft.
